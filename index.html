APR

Annual Percentage Rate - The annual rate charged for borrowing or earned through an investment, including fees.

Assets

Resources with economic value that an individual or corporation owns or controls with the expectation of future benefit.

Amortization

The process of spreading out a loan into a series of fixed payments over time.

Annuity

A financial product that pays out fixed payments to an individual, typically used as retirement income.

Arbitrage

The simultaneous purchase and sale of an asset to profit from a difference in the price.

Appreciation

An increase in the value of an asset over time.

Bankruptcy

A legal proceeding involving a person or business that is unable to repay outstanding debts.

Bear Market

A market condition in which prices are falling, encouraging selling.

Balance Sheet

A financial statement that reports a company's assets, liabilities and shareholders' equity at a specific point in time.

Bull Market

A market condition in which prices are rising or expected to rise.

Bond

A fixed income instrument that represents a loan made by an investor to a borrower.

Blue Chip

A nationally recognized, well-established, and financially sound company.

Credit Score

A number between 300-850 that depicts a consumer's creditworthiness based on their credit history.

Compound Interest

Interest calculated on the initial principal and also on the accumulated interest of previous periods.

Capital Gains

The profit from the sale of property or an investment, calculated by subtracting the purchase price from the sale price.

Collateral

An asset that a borrower offers as a way for a lender to secure the loan.

Cash Flow

The net amount of cash and cash-equivalents moving into and out of a business.

Commodity

A basic good used in commerce that is interchangeable with other goods of the same type.

Dividend

A distribution of a portion of a company's earnings, decided by the board of directors, to a class of its shareholders.

Diversification

A risk management strategy that mixes a wide variety of investments within a portfolio.

Debt-to-Income Ratio

A personal finance measure that compares an individual's debt payment to their overall income.

Depreciation

A decrease in the value of an asset over time due to wear and tear or obsolescence.

Derivative

A financial security with a value that is reliant upon or derived from an underlying asset or group of assets.

Default

The failure to repay a debt including interest or principal on a loan or security.

Equity

The value of an asset minus the value of all liabilities on that asset.

Exchange Rate

The value of one currency for the purpose of conversion to another.

ETF

Exchange-Traded Fund - A type of security that tracks an index, sector, commodity, or other asset but trades like a stock.

Escrow

A financial arrangement where a third party holds and regulates payment of funds for two parties in a transaction.

Earnings Per Share (EPS)

A company's profit divided by the number of outstanding shares of its common stock.

Expense Ratio

The annual fee that all funds or ETFs charge their shareholders.

Fiscal Year

A one-year period that companies and governments use for financial reporting and budgeting.

Fiat Currency

Government-issued currency that is not backed by a physical commodity but by the government that issued it.

Foreclosure

A legal process by which a lender attempts to recover the balance of a loan from a borrower who has stopped making payments.

Futures

Financial contracts obligating the buyer to purchase an asset or the seller to sell an asset at a predetermined future date and price.

Fixed Income

A type of investment that provides a return in the form of fixed periodic payments and eventual return of principal.

Float

The number of shares of a company's stock that are available for trading by the public.
